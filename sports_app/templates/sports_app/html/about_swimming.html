Introduction: Swimming is both a competitive sport and a valuable life skill, involving the movement of the body through water using various techniques. It is enjoyed by millions around the world for fitness, recreation, and competition.

History: Swimming dates back to prehistoric times, with cave paintings depicting swimmers from 7,000 years ago. It became a formal sport in the 19th century, with the first Olympic swimming competitions held in 1896 for men and 1912 for women.

Basic Rules:

  • Competitions are governed by FINA rules.
  • Main strokes: freestyle, backstroke, breaststroke, and butterfly.
  • Swimmers must stay in their assigned lanes.
  • Specific stroke techniques must be followed.
  • The fastest swimmer to touch the wall wins.

Famous Events:

  • Olympic Games
  • FINA World Championships
  • Commonwealth Games
  • Pan Pacific Swimming Championships

Fun Facts:

  • Michael Phelps is the most decorated Olympian of all time with 23 gold medals.
  • Swimming burns more calories than running due to water resistance.
